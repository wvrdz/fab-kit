## Here are the things that I like about each of these packages

SpecKit

I feel very at home with SpecKit. The commands are intuitive, the file locations are great. 
The stages are obvious. It's very easy to find out where you are currently.
It's not very easy to find out which stage you are in, but I don't think that's a problem. 
I love the fact that SpecKit is so easy to modify to your own needs. 
There is no inbuilt way of creating a centralized spec.
I love the fact that you can modify the folder naming scheme. I have completely modded the way folders are named in the spec folder. 
I love the fact that this is completely independent of anything that needs to be installed from a system. It's a pure prompt play. 

OPSX

I love the `fast-forward` command. 
I love the idea that after every work, you can archive it and the specs are hydrated into a centralized spec. 
I love the idea of having separate changes and separate specs. 

What I don't like about it is the inability to find out what you are doing currently very easily or which stage you are in. 
I don't like the fact that all the commands are pretty much fixed and are difficult to change. 
Love the idea that the workflow uses skills more than commands, given that skills are more interchangeable and usable by the agent. Especially with something like Fast Forward, that would be really useful. 
Provides lesser control over the folder naming scheme.
